Lost everything to get freedom

2022-06-25 4 min read # freedom

Recently, I has been watching a movie named Fight Club, there is a sentence that impressed me, "It's only after we've lost everything that we're free to do anything."

Think it carefully, the condition and result of this sentence is hard to achieve, it is an ideal situation. We can't lost everything, and we are unable to get absolute freedom. Firstly, I don't understand it until one day when I was in the shower and remembered an incident from ten years ago.

In 2010, with the help of my uncle, I successfully registered my first QQ account when QQ was so popular. There were some people who made efforts to upgrade level quickly and decorate their own QQ Show......Because it was my first time to use QQ, I was so interested in it that I thought it was important to me. One day, I found I can't login my QQ account, did someone steal my account? I tried my best to make my account back, but I failed. I was so sad, because there were many things that I thought important on QQ, such as the game data of Roco kindom, and my QQ friends. Although I would register a new one, I was still sad. However, luckily, I found my account, I just forgot the password, and now, I has been using it.

At that time, was the QQ number a kind of bondage for me? If I hadn't had it, I probably wouldn't have been so upset for so long about all the games, levels and other virtual things that I couldn't abandon in a sense. I don't think it would have mattered if I had cancelled my QQ number now.

The more you carry on your back, the harder it is to walk. The ideal state is to be relaxed and absolutely free, but reality does not give us absolute freedom, we cannot leave everything behind, there are many attachments and ties, we can only approach this ideal state step by step.

We can only move closer to this ideal state one step at a time. People often talk about "cutting off", cutting off all those things that are unnecessary, inappropriate and outdated, and cutting off our attachment to them, so that we can live a simple and refreshing life after "cutting off".

I look at the hundreds of apps installed on my phone, but what I actually use every day are only social apps such as WeChat and Weibo, and a few apps related to study and work, but why do I still have so many installed? Because we don't know when we'll use it next, so it's better to keep it in there than to install it again when the time comes.

It's a good idea to empty your mind of the things you need to leave behind and keep the people and things you want to treasure.
